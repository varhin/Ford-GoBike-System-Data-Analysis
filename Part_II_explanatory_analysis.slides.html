Investigation Overview¶

My focus for the presentation is on what are the peak days and hours for the bike sharing system. I will introduce the day variable(for start time), followed by the hours in day, then plot the relationship between these two variables using a heat map.

Also, I will introduce user type variable as a single variable. Then I will compare user type with ride duration and also days in the week to determine what user uses the bike system the most.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area.

Distribution of Trip Start Day¶

Trips taken at bike stations are spread across various days of the week. The lowest number of rides are taken on weekends which is about 15,000 rides and most trips are taken during weekdays with the highest number of rides at about 35,000.

Distribution of Trip Start Hour¶

Hourly count of bike rides helps determine what the peak hours are in a day. The peak hours are 7am - 9am and 4pm - 6pm with the highest count of more than 40,000 in the mornings.

Day vrs Hour¶

Plotting a heatmap to show the relationship between starting time hour and day. The heatmap supports the analysis that were made in the previous two chart for day and hour. The portions shade yellow and green matches with the days and hours that were shown in the above graphs. The peaks days and hours are Tuesday, Wednesdays and Thurdays between the hours of 7am-9am and 4pm-6pm (ie. 16-18). This is probably so, because most people might be going to or returning from work or school during these times.
